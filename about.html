About Seth Perkins


The Advocate

Seth has spent his entire adult life working in advocacy. Whether for our brothers and sisters living on the street, or for our children, or our environment, or now in the field of mental health. Seth currently runs a respite home for folks transitioning out of psychiatric facilities and prisons. In a way, Seth has always had a job provided by the material and psychological effects of capitalism. He has seen first hand the trauma inflicted upon the most vulnerable of society, and it has led him to his decision to seek public office. He will be beholden to the working people of our district and state, not to the millionaires and billionaires. The same cannot be said about others on Beacon Hill.

The Union Leader

Seth is a proud member of, and steward for, the Service Employees International Union. SEIU represents laborers in essential services such as hospital workers, home care and nursing home workers, public service (including education) workers; and property services workers. Members of the SEIU have been on the front lines of this pandemic and understand the material necessities required to successfully combat it. These workers have staved us off from utter chaos through solidarity, not bosse’ orders. Turns out the most efficient way to handle a crisis is teamwork. In Massachusetts, we are faced with several crises: the opioid crisis, the mental health crisis, the housing crisis, the environmental crisis, and the educational crisis, to name a few. The solution to all of these is, yet again, teamwork. The only way to tackle the problems facing our Commonwealth is the strategy of Union: greater communal participation and popular control of resources. Seth wants to apply the Union model across our Commonwealth because neither privatization nor centralized authority has resolved our serious problems.

The Minuteman

Seth was born and raised in Massachusetts, and apart from a service year with Americorps, has lived here his entire life. Originally from Gloucester, Seth spent time in Western Mass attending the University of Massachusetts, where he studied political science and history. Wherever he has gone, Seth has held the proud title of “neighbor” and has deeply invested his time in the interests of those around him. He has always been committed to the notion of the Commonwealth, or the revolutionary idea that we are obligated to care for one another simply because we share the same street. Or the same neighborhood. Or the same city. Or the same state. We are necessarily interconnected, and neighborliness is an act of revolution.


Seth excitedly enrolled as a Democrat at age 18 and stayed loyal until the defeat of Donald Trump. Seth thinks fondly of Democrats despite his exiting the party. Growing up, Seth had a modest background that became much more modest after the death of his father during the 2008 financial crisis. His mother, a house cleaner, and his brother scraped by the best they could. Thanks to many good Democratic families, and some apolitical folk, Seth was taken care of. He became a Democrat because he saw their benevolence firsthand and believed in the system. However, as of recent it appears that the system itself is incorrect. Democrats are good capitalists, but Seth is no longer a capitalist. Regardless of political affiliation, local politics is about fighting for those you love, for your home, and for your neighbor. That’s Seth’s goal: to be a minuteman on behalf of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.
